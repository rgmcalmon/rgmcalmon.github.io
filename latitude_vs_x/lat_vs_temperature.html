Max Temperature

Temperature is clearly highest near the equator, and it dips predictably as one travels further away from the equator. The temperature distribution is not centered perfectly around the equator, but this is to be expected due to the tilt of the earth. A quadratic curve has been fit to the data in order to illustrate the unimodality of the distribution, as well as to pinpoint roughly where the temperature is maximized. The above data was taken less than a month after the spring equinox of 2019, and the temperature is maximized roughly 3.5 degrees south of the equator, indicating that the north hemisphere is slowly warming up and has not caught up with the south hemisphere yet.
